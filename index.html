Abstract

Industrial Video Anomaly Detection (IVAD) is crucial for modern manufacturing and quality control, yet existing VLM-based anomaly-reasoning methods decline in industrial settings characterized by intricate object transformations, strict physics, and procedural constraints. We introduce O-VAD, a training-free agentic framework that detects anomalies free of any domain-specific knowledge, labels, or predefined taxonomy — emphasizing object state evolution like human inspectors. Rather than injecting external knowledge or relying on complex prompt design, O-VAD lets the VLM build its own understanding through a “ground → track → reason” pipeline: it tracks the spatiotemporal dynamics and underlying transformations of detected objects over time, then reasons over per-object temporal state trajectories to identify abnormal objects in grounded frames. Extensive experiments on three IVAD datasets show that O-VAD outperforms frontier VLMs, agentic frameworks, and traditional VAD methods fine-tuned on the respective datasets, while providing interpretable reports over anomaly processes and types.

Method

A training-free “ground → track → reason” agentic pipeline that shifts the unit of analysis from holistic frames to individual objects.

STAGE 01

Automated Object Grounding

Samples candidate frames spanning the video and prompts a VLM to discover every task-relevant object, then segments each with SAM 3 to produce initial masks and structured metadata — no manual annotation.

STAGE 02

Object-Centric State Tracking

Constructs spatiotemporal tubelets, recovers tracks lost to transformations via proximity and semantic priors, and queries the VLM at temporal transitions for open-ended state-change detection and inter-object interaction analysis.

STAGE 03

Human-like Anomaly Reasoning

A cascaded chain-of-thought separates expected process actions from failure outcomes, then visually verifies candidate anomalies — yielding a structured report with type, severity, grounded frames, affected objects, and a causal explanation.

CASE 1 · PLASTIC BOTTLE ROTATION

Loss of containment

A clamped bottle leaks severely at frames 60–80. GPT-5 reports “no leakage.” O-VAD tracks progressive deformation (0–60), detects material release at frame 60, and classifies a high-severity loss-of-containment anomaly — attributing it to repeated clamp-induced stress.

CASE 2 · HINGE SCREW FASTENING

Cross-object diagnosis

Three defects co-occur: repeated screw back-out (30–110), head-slot stripping (80–90), and screwdriver shaft bending (60–100). GPT-5 concludes normal tightening; O-VAD captures all three streams and synthesizes them into a coherent torque-control / bit-mismatch failure hypothesis.

Ablations

Removing one module at a time on four Phys-AD subsets (sticky roller, liquid, screw, rubber band) isolates each component's contribution.

−100%

State tracking is indispensable

Removing it collapses the system to “all normal” — P/R/F1 fall to zero on three of four subsets. BERT scores stay high on fluent-but-empty output, showing text metrics alone can't judge detection.

−0.25

Captioning anchors the scene

Video-level captions supply a global semantic prior. Removing them drops AUC by up to 0.247 on holistic-behavior anomalies, while localized defects (screw) are unaffected.

+0.09

Structured CoT calibrates

The cascaded six-step reasoning chain lifts AUC by 0.038–0.091 over a generic “think step by step” prompt and yields more faithful, better-calibrated explanations.

−0.18

Verification safeguards

Multiplicative confidence gating re-examines borderline candidates against evidence frames. Removing it degrades AUC by up to 0.178 and sharpens true-vs-ambiguous separation.
